# Editing your website — a friendly guide

Hi Lerly! This is your website's built-in editor. With it you can add new
paintings, mark whether you're taking orders, and change any of the words on
your site — no coding, and nothing can break. This guide walks you through it in
plain language. Take your time; you can't hurt anything.

Whenever you save, **your site updates on its own in about 2 minutes.** That's
just how long it takes the internet to catch up — refresh the page after a couple
of minutes and your change will be there.

---

## One-time setup: get your access key (about 5 minutes)

Your editor needs a private "key" so it knows it's really you making changes.
You only have to make this once. Keep it somewhere safe, like your password
manager or notes app.

1. Go to **https://github.com/settings/personal-access-tokens/new** (log in with
   the GitHub account that owns your site if it asks).
2. Under **Token name**, type something you'll recognize, like `Website editor`.
3. Under **Expiration**, pick a length you're comfortable with. When it expires,
   you'll just make a new one the same way.
4. Under **Repository access**, choose **Only select repositories**, and pick
   **lerlylizstudios.github.io**. (This makes the key work *only* for your site —
   the safest option.)
5. Under **Permissions → Repository permissions**, find **Contents** and set it
   to **Read and write**. Leave everything else as "No access".
6. Click **Generate token** at the bottom, then **copy** the key it shows you
   (it starts with `github_pat_`). This is the only time it's shown, so paste it
   somewhere safe right away.

> **Why set it up this way?** Choosing this one website and "Contents" only means
> that even if the key ever leaked, it could only edit *this* website's content —
> never your account, other projects, or settings. Never share the key or paste
> it anywhere except your own editor page.

That's it. You won't need to do this again until the key expires.

---

## Logging in

1. Go to your website and add **`#admin`** to the end of the address, like:
   `https://lerlylizstudios.github.io/#admin`
   (Bookmark that page so it's easy to get back to.)
2. Paste your access key into the box and click **Log in**.

Your key is never saved on the website or anywhere online — it lives only in your
browser for as long as the page is open. If you close the tab or click **Log
out**, you'll paste it again next time. That's on purpose, and it keeps your site
secure.

## Adding a painting

1. Click the **Paintings** tab.
2. In **Add a painting**:
   - **Photo of the painting** — pick a photo from your computer or phone. Any
     size is fine; the editor shrinks and optimizes it for you automatically, so
     your site stays fast.
   - **Pet's name** — this shows under the painting (e.g. *Bella*).
   - **Category** — Dogs, Cats, Ornaments, or Studies.
   - **Describe the painting for screen readers** — a short sentence describing
     what the painting looks like, for visitors who use screen readers and for
     Google. This one is required. Example: *"Golden retriever with a blue
     background, painted in warm oranges."*
3. Click **Add painting**. It appears in **Your paintings** below.
4. Click **Save changes** at the bottom when you're done.

**Want a painting shown big on your homepage?** In the list, click **Show on
Home** on that painting. Only one can be featured at a time — the starred one
(★ On Home) is the current pick.

---

## Editing, reordering, and removing paintings

In the **Your paintings** list, each painting has:

- Boxes to change its **name**, **category**, and **description** — just type.
- **↑ / ↓** arrows to move it earlier or later in your gallery.
- **✕** to remove it (it asks you to confirm first, so you can't delete by
  accident).

Always click **Save changes** when you're finished.

---

## Marking commissions open or closed

1. Click the **Commissions** tab.
2. Choose **Open — taking orders** or **Booked — waitlist only**.
   - *Open* shows "Order on Etsy" buttons across your site.
   - *Booked* switches them to "Join the waitlist."
3. Click **Save changes**.

This one setting updates the little badge and the buttons everywhere on your
site at once.

---

## Editing your words

- The **Commissions tab** also lets you edit the **listing description** (the
  paragraph under "About this piece") and the three **how-it-works** steps.
- **About tab** — your artist statement, your photo (upload it the same easy way
  as a painting), and the small "materials" line.
- **FAQ tab** — add, edit, reorder (↑/↓), or remove (✕) questions and answers.

Type your changes, then **Save changes**. Same as always.

---

## Good to know

- **Every save updates your live site in about 2 minutes.** If you don't see a
  change right away, wait a moment and refresh.
- **You can make lots of changes and save once.** Add three paintings, flip
  commissions, fix a typo — then hit Save a single time.
- **If something goes wrong**, the editor tells you in plain English at the
  bottom of the screen. Usually it just means the access key expired — make a new
  one (see the setup steps) and log in again.
- **You truly can't break the site.** If a save doesn't go through, nothing
  changes and your site stays exactly as it was.

Happy painting! 🎨
